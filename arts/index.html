Painting

I learned to paint when I was a child with my mother who used to paint in the afternoons. I started with classical oil painting, one day I discovered the abstract and fell in love, then I discovered other techniques such as watercolor, pastely acrylic but finally encaustic won me over.

Sculpture

The first sculpture I made was totally abstract, I made it for fun with the unicel packaging that came in a television box. One day there was a collectors meeting at my house, one told me what the price was, I was surprised and told him: you put a price on it, he took his checkbook and paid me. Then in San Miguel de Allende, Mexico. I learned the technique of ceramics that became my passion. I love to see how the earth is transformed into works of art.

Engraving

Fate led me to live for a time in San Miguel de Allende, Mexico. There I met many internationally famous artists, over time I learned about their techniques and one day I decided to try engraving, to everyone's surprise I liked it so much that a few weeks later I was already mounting my first exhibition. Engraving traps thoughts and emotions and leaves them forever between metal and paper.
